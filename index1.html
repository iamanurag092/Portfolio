Healthcare AI September 2024

Why I Built an AI Medical Imaging System

The intersection of AI and healthcare represents one of the most impactful applications of machine learning. Here's why I chose pneumonia detection as my entry point.

The Problem Space

Pneumonia affects millions globally, with diagnosis speed being critical for patient outcomes. Traditional radiological diagnosis requires specialized expertise and time‚Äîboth of which are scarce in resource-constrained settings. I wanted to build something that could genuinely save lives, not just demonstrate technical capability.

Technical Decisions

  • CNN Architecture: Leveraged convolutional neural networks for proven effectiveness in image classification, particularly where spatial hierarchies matter.
  • TensorFlow: Selected for superior deployment ecosystem and production-grade tooling essential for healthcare applications.
  • Vultr Cloud: Prioritized low-latency inference over cost, as real-time diagnosis is critical in emergency scenarios.
  • Data Augmentation: Extensive preprocessing improved model robustness with limited medical data.

Impact & Learning

Ranking 35th globally validated my approach, but more importantly, this project taught me that AI engineering is about understanding domain constraints, optimizing for real-world deployment, and maintaining ethical responsibility in high-stakes applications.

NLP & GenAI November 2024

Building Privacy-First AI Systems

How I combined semantic search with local LLMs to create a zero-cost, privacy-preserving AI recruitment platform.

The Motivation

Traditional ATS systems rely on keyword matching‚Äîfundamentally flawed and missing semantic context. Modern AI solutions often compromise privacy or incur prohibitive costs. I set out to prove ethical AI doesn't require trade-offs.

Architecture Choices

  • SentenceTransformers: Enabled semantic understanding beyond keywords without training overhead.
  • Local LLaMA-3: Hosted generative AI locally ensuring zero API costs and complete data privacy.
  • Hybrid Ranking: Combined semantic similarity with skill coverage for nuanced recommendations.

Why Privacy Matters

Career documents contain deeply personal information. By processing everything locally, users maintain complete control. This is an ethical stance on how AI should respect user privacy, especially with sensitive information.

Predictive Analytics December 2024

Engineering a Churn Prediction System

Understanding why customers leave is crucial for business survival. Building an end-to-end ML system that turns data into actionable retention strategies.

Business Context First

Customer acquisition costs 5-25x more than retention. For telecom companies, churn prediction isn't just analytics‚Äîit's survival. I approached this as a business problem first, ML problem second.

Engineering Decisions

  • XGBoost: Superior performance on tabular data with built-in feature importance crucial for stakeholder understanding.
  • PostgreSQL ETL: Robust pipelines for multi-source integration because real-world data is never in one place.
  • SHAP Explainability: Businesses need to understand predictions to take action.

Deployment Lessons

Real-time prediction APIs need speed (~300ms), reliability, and scalability. Through performance profiling and optimization, I built a complete pipeline from data ingestion to prediction delivery accessible to non-technical teams.

Philosophy January 2025

Production Over Perfection

My AI/ML engineering philosophy: why I prioritize deployable solutions over theoretical optimization.

Ship It, Then Improve It

Early on, I spent weeks optimizing for marginal gains while neglecting deployment. A deployed model at 85% creates more value than 95% accuracy that never ships.

Core Principles

  • Problem First: Understand business needs before choosing algorithms. Sometimes simple heuristics beat complex neural networks.
  • Production Constraints: Latency, privacy, and cost drive better decisions than unconstrained research.
  • Explainability Required: Stakeholders need to trust AI systems. Black boxes are academically interesting but practically limited.

What's Next

Exploring reinforcement learning, multimodal AI, and edge deployment. The goal remains: build AI that solves real problems for real people.
