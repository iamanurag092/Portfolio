Healthcare AI September 2024

Why I Built an AI Medical Imaging System: Bridging Technology and Healthcare

The intersection of AI and healthcare represents one of the most impactful applications of machine learning. Here's why I chose pneumonia detection as my entry point into medical AI.

The Problem Space

Pneumonia affects millions globally, with diagnosis speed being critical for patient outcomes. Traditional radiological diagnosis requires specialized expertise and time‚Äîboth of which are scarce in resource-constrained healthcare settings. I wanted to build something that could genuinely save lives, not just demonstrate technical capability.

Technical Decisions

  • CNN Architecture: Chose convolutional neural networks for their proven effectiveness in image classification tasks, particularly medical imaging where spatial hierarchies matter.
  • TensorFlow over PyTorch: Selected for its superior deployment ecosystem and production-grade tooling, essential for healthcare applications.
  • Vultr Cloud Deployment: Prioritized low-latency inference (<500ms) over cost, as real-time diagnosis is critical in emergency scenarios.
  • Image Augmentation: Implemented extensive preprocessing (rotation, flip, zoom, noise injection) to improve model robustness with limited medical data.

Challenges Overcome

The biggest challenge was balancing model accuracy with inference speed. Medical contexts demand both precision and real-time capability. Through model optimization, quantization, and efficient preprocessing pipelines, I achieved sub-500ms latency without sacrificing the 89% accuracy threshold.

Impact & Learning

Ranking 35th globally among 6000+ teams validated my approach, but more importantly, this project taught me that AI engineering isn't just about algorithms‚Äîit's about understanding domain constraints, optimizing for real-world deployment, and maintaining ethical responsibility in high-stakes applications.

NLP & GenAI November 2024

Building Privacy-First AI: The Resume Screening Platform Journey

How I combined semantic search with local LLMs to create a zero-cost, privacy-preserving AI recruitment platform that challenges the status quo.

The Motivation

Traditional ATS systems rely on keyword matching‚Äîa fundamentally flawed approach that misses semantic context and penalizes diverse backgrounds. Meanwhile, modern AI solutions often compromise user privacy or incur prohibitive API costs. I set out to prove that ethical AI doesn't require trade-offs.

Architecture Choices

  • SentenceTransformers: Leveraged pre-trained models for semantic similarity without training overhead, enabling understanding of context beyond keywords.
  • Local LLaMA-3 via Ollama: Hosted generative AI locally for resume optimization, ensuring zero API costs and complete data privacy‚Äîcritical for sensitive career documents.
  • Hybrid Ranking System: Combined semantic similarity scores with skill coverage analysis for more nuanced job recommendations than either approach alone.
  • SQLite: Chose for simplicity and portability, prioritizing rapid iteration over enterprise-scale database complexity.

Key Innovations

The skill gap analysis engine was particularly challenging to implement. It required parsing job descriptions, extracting required skills, comparing against candidate profiles, and generating actionable recommendations‚Äîall while maintaining semantic understanding rather than simple string matching.

Why Privacy Matters

Career documents contain deeply personal information. By processing everything locally, users maintain complete control of their data. This isn't just a technical decision‚Äîit's an ethical stance on how AI should respect user privacy, especially in systems that handle sensitive information.

Predictive Analytics December 2024

From Data to Decisions: Engineering a Churn Prediction System

Understanding why customers leave is crucial for business survival. Here's how I built an end-to-end ML system that turns data into actionable retention strategies.

Business Context First

Customer acquisition costs 5-25x more than retention. For telecom companies with tight margins, churn prediction isn't just analytics‚Äîit's survival. I approached this as a business problem first, ML problem second.

Engineering Decisions

  • XGBoost: Selected for its superior performance on tabular data and built-in feature importance, crucial for business stakeholders who need to understand "why" behind predictions.
  • PostgreSQL ETL: Designed robust pipelines for multi-source integration (customer data, transactions, interactions) because real-world data is never in one place.
  • Docker Containerization: Ensured consistent deployment across dev, staging, and production environments, reducing setup time by 80%.
  • SHAP Explainability: Implemented model interpretability because businesses need to understand predictions to take action.

The Explainability Challenge

Achieving 85%+ ROC-AUC was important, but explaining predictions to non-technical stakeholders was harder than building the model. SHAP values transformed black-box predictions into actionable insights: "This customer is high-risk because of low engagement and recent billing issues."

Deployment Lessons

Real-time prediction APIs need to be fast (~300ms), reliable, and scalable. Through performance profiling, database indexing, and efficient model serialization, I optimized the full pipeline from data ingestion to prediction delivery. The Streamlit dashboard made insights accessible to business teams without technical expertise.

Technical Philosophy January 2025

My AI/ML Engineering Philosophy: Production Over Perfection

Reflecting on lessons learned across projects and why I prioritize deployable solutions over theoretical optimization.

Ship It, Then Improve It

Early in my journey, I spent weeks optimizing models for marginal accuracy gains while neglecting deployment. I learned that a deployed model at 85% accuracy creates more value than a 95% accurate model that never ships.

Core Principles

  • Start with the problem, not the algorithm: Understand business needs before choosing ML techniques. Sometimes a simple heuristic beats a complex neural network.
  • Production constraints are features: Latency requirements, privacy concerns, and cost limitations drive better architectural decisions than unconstrained research.
  • Explainability is not optional: Stakeholders need to trust and understand AI systems. Black boxes may be academically interesting but practically limited.
  • Local-first when possible: Cloud services are powerful but introduce dependencies, costs, and privacy concerns. Local deployment maximizes control.

Technical Skills That Matter

Beyond ML algorithms, these skills define production-ready AI engineering: Docker for consistent deployment, Flask/FastAPI for serving models, PostgreSQL for data pipelines, Git for version control, and cloud platforms for scaling. Theory is important, but engineering discipline turns models into products.

What's Next

I'm exploring reinforcement learning for dynamic systems, multimodal AI combining vision and language, and edge deployment for resource-constrained environments. The goal remains constant: build AI that solves real problems for real people.
